The central trade

Spend inexpensive decode work to avoid expensive memory movement.

ChromoFold is most promising when the workload is capacity-bound, bandwidth-bound, sparse, or highly repetitive. It can lose when decode overhead, poor locality or metadata pressure dominates.

See where it wins and loses
saved allocation + saved HBM traffic > decode overhead

This is the engineering condition. It is not a universal performance promise.

Local-first workflow

A recommendation becomes a claim only after six gates.

1

Inspect

Capture the exact machine, driver, CUDA and runtime.

2

Configure

Generate a profile with silent fallback disabled.

3

Baseline

Run the original workload with identical inputs.

4

Compare

Measure capacity, latency, throughput, peak VRAM and correctness.

5

Qualify

Bind PASS, FAIL or INCOMPLETE evidence to the exact release digest.

6

Publish honestly

Keep independent reproduction and repository qualification separate.

Project principles

Local first

Machine inspection and workload details stay on the user’s machine.

No silent fallback

Unsupported compressed paths fail visibly rather than pretending to work.

Digest-bound proof

Qualification follows the immutable artifact, runtime and workload fingerprint.

Negative results remain

FAIL, INCOMPLETE and slower regions are retained as product evidence.
