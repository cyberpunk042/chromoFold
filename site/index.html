Frequently asked

What ChromoFold is—and is not.

Is this just ordinary compression?

No. The central goal is to keep selected serving data compact while the consuming GPU operation decodes values at use time, avoiding a full temporary tensor.

Does ChromoFold make every workload faster?

No. It targets memory-bound, capacity-bound, sparse and highly repetitive workloads. Decode overhead can lose when avoided data is small or the kernel is compute-bound.

Are the headline results production-qualified?

The public registry currently labels repository kernel benchmarks as measured. Production claims require exact-runtime, exact-digest hardware qualification.

Does the public planner inspect my computer?

No. It runs entirely in the browser. Real machine inspection is performed only by the downloaded local CLI and Hub.
